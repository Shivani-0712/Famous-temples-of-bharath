Meenakshi Temple

Location: Madurai, Tamil Nadu

Deity: Goddess Meenakshi (Parvati) and Lord Sundareshwarar (Shiva)

Famous For: Towering gopurams and vibrant sculptures in Dravidian style

Meenakshi Temple Details

Complex Size: Covers 14 acres with 14 gopurams; tallest ~170 feet

Historical Era: Originally from the 6th century CE, current structure mostly 12th–17th centuries

Unique Feature: Celebrates female divinity with Meenakshi as the primary deity

Festival: Meenakshi Thirukalyanam during the Chithirai Festival (April)

Cultural Role: Major spiritual and cultural center in South India

Brihadeeswarar Temple

Location: Thanjavur, Tamil Nadu

Deity: Lord Shiva

Also Known As: Peruvudaiyar Kovil or Big Temple

Brihadeeswarar Temple Details

Built By: Raja Raja Chola I in 1010 AD

UNESCO Status: Part of the Great Living Chola Temples

Architecture: Masterpiece of Chola style; Vimana ~66 meters tall

Nandi Statue: One of India’s largest monolithic Nandis (~6 meters long)

Unique Feature: The temple tower's shadow never falls on the ground at noon

Cultural Importance: Represents Tamil heritage, temple engineering, and devotion
