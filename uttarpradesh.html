Kashi Vishwanath Temple

Location: Varanasi (Kashi), Uttar Pradesh, on the banks of the Ganga River

Deity: Lord Shiva (as Vishwanath, “Lord of the Universe”)

Significance: One of the 12 Jyotirlingas; among the most sacred Shiva shrines in India

Kashi Vishwanath Temple Details

Spiritual Importance: Believed that a visit grants moksha (liberation)

Architecture: Gold-plated shikhara (spire); recently expanded via the Kashi Vishwanath Corridor

Mythology: Said to be where Shiva resides eternally

Daily Rituals: Includes Mangala Aarti, Rudrabhishek, and evening Ganga Aarti nearby

Festival: Maha Shivaratri celebrated with great devotion

Varanasi Banke Bihari Temple

Location: Varanasi, Uttar Pradesh

Deity: Banke Bihari, a form of Lord Krishna known for divine playfulness

Architecture: Simpler compared to its Vrindavan counterpart; serves local devotees

Banke Bihari Temple Details

Rituals: Bhajans, aartis, and Krishna-centric festivals like Janmashtami celebrated
