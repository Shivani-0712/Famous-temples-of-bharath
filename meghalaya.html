Nartiang Durga Temple

Location: Nartiang village, West Jaintia Hills, Meghalaya

Deity: Goddess Durga

Significance: One of the 51 Shakti Peethas; believed to be where Sati’s left thigh fell

Cultural Fusion: Blend of Hindu and Khasi tribal rituals

Nartiang Durga Temple Details

Built By: Kings of the Jaintia Kingdom after converting to Hinduism

Festivals: Durga Puja celebrated with tribal animal sacrifice and Hindu customs

Architecture: Simple stone structure with indigenous Khasi elements

Priests: A Khasi tribal priest and a Hindu Brahmin jointly perform the rituals

Mahadev Khola Dham Temple

Location: Shillong, Meghalaya (near Umshyrpi River)

Deity: Lord Shiva

Origin: Discovered by a Gorkha Subedar after a divine dream

Architecture: Built in North Indian style inside a cave

Mahadev Khola Dham Temple Details

Historical Link: Associated with sage Lakhiya Baba

Age: Approx. 150 years old

Belief: Cave said to connect with Kamakhya Temple in Assam

Festival: Maha Shivaratri – attracts over 25,000 devotees

Timings: Open 6:00 AM–12:00 PM and 4:30 PM–6:00 PM
