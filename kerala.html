Sabarimala Temple

Location: Sabarimala, Periyar Tiger Reserve, Pathanamthitta, Kerala

Deity: Lord Ayyappa (son of Shiva and Mohini)

Significance: One of the largest annual pilgrimages in the world

Sabarimala Temple Details

Unique Practices: 41-day vratham before pilgrimage; traditional restriction on women aged 10–50

Pilgrimage Route: Devotees walk through forests to reach the shrine

Iconic Symbol: Chanting “Swamiye Saranam Ayyappa”

Sacred Spot: 18 holy steps (Pathinettam Padi) to the sanctum

Festival: Makaravilakku — celestial light sighting and massive gathering

Guruvayur Temple

Location: Guruvayur, Thrissur district, Kerala

Deity: Guruvayurappan (Lord Krishna in child form)

Significance: Called “Dwarka of the South” and “Bhuloka Vaikunta”

Guruvayur Temple Details

Architecture: Traditional Kerala style with gold-plated flagstaff, wood carvings

Rituals: Udayasthamana Pooja (sunrise to sunset), Thulabharam, Bhasma Aarti

Festivals: Krishna Janmashtami, Guruvayur Ekadasi

Elephant Sanctuary: Over 50 elephants maintained for temple processions

Management: Guruvayur Devaswom Board

Entry Rules: Hindus only, with strict dress codes
