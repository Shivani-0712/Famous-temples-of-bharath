Jagannath Temple

Location: Puri, Odisha (Eastern India)

Deities: Lord Jagannath (form of Vishnu/Krishna), Subhadra, and Balabhadra

Architecture: Kalinga style; tall tower with a chakra on top

Jagannath Temple Details

Significance: One of the Char Dham pilgrimage sites (along with Badrinath, Dwarka, and Rameswaram)

Unique Feature: Deities made of wood and replaced every 12–19 years in a ritual called Nabakalebara

Mythology: Lord Jagannath is worshipped with a tribal touch

Famous Festival: Rath Yatra (Chariot Festival) – massive annual procession

Lingaraj Temple

Location: Bhubaneswar, Odisha

Deity: Lord Shiva (worshiped as Harihara – fusion of Shiva and Vishnu)

Architecture: Classic Kalinga style – 55-meter tall spire, intricately carved

Lingaraj Temple Details

Built In: 11th century by Somavamsi rulers

Spiritual Importance: Major Shaivite temple; spiritual hub of Bhubaneswar

Special Feature: Non-Hindus are not allowed inside the temple

Festivals Celebrated: Shivratri, Rukuna Rath Yatra
