Mahabodhi Temple

Location: Bodh Gaya, Bihar

Significance: Site where Gautama Buddha attained enlightenment under the Bodhi tree

Deity: Not a deity-based temple; it is a Buddhist pilgrimage site

Mahabodhi Temple Details

Original Construction: Built by Emperor Ashoka in the 3rd century BCE

Current Structure: Dates to the 5th–6th century CE, restored over time

Architecture: Classic brick-built Indian temple with a tall pyramidal spire

UNESCO Status: Declared a World Heritage Site in 2002

Sacred Elements: Includes the Bodhi Tree, Vajrasana (Diamond Throne), and Buddha’s statue

Pilgrimage Importance: One of the four holiest Buddhist sites globally

Vishnupad Temple

Location: Gaya, Bihar

Deity: Lord Vishnu

Significance: Temple enshrines Vishnu's footprint

Vishnupad Temple Details

Rituals: Famous for Pind Daan (ancestral offerings)

Architecture: Traditional temple design with a tall shikhara (spire)

Legends: Associated with Lord Vishnu's Varaha avatar

Religious Importance: Considered one of the 108 Divya Desams

Festivals: Celebrated during Vaikuntha Ekadashi and Pitru Paksha
