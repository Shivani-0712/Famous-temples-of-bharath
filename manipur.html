Shri Govindajee Temple

Location: Near the Royal Palace, Imphal, Manipur

Deity: Lord Govindajee (form of Krishna), with Radha, Balabhadra, and Subhadra

Architecture: Traditional Vaishnavite style with twin gold domes and a large mandapa

Shri Govindajee Temple Details

Built By: Maharaja Nara Singh in 1846 AD

Significance: Main Vaishnavite temple of Manipur, historically linked to the royal family

Festivals: Janmashtami and Rasa Lila with Manipuri dance and devotion

Cultural Role: Center for Vaishnavite arts, music, and dance in the state

Sanamahi Temple

Location: Imphal, near Kangla Fort

Deity: Lainingthou Sanamahi – household deity of the Meitei people

Architecture: Indigenous Meitei style – simple and elegant

Sanamahi Temple Details

Religion: Sanamahism – traditional Meitei faith

Significance: Sacred to Meitei people; predates Hinduism in Manipur

Cultural Importance: Symbol of revival and preservation of Meitei culture

Festivals: Sanamahi Ahong Khong Chingba – a grand cultural procession
